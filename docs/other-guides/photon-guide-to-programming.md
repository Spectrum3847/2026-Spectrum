# Photon Guide to Programming

This guide outlines the philosophy and recommended approaches for members of Spectrum, often referred to as "Photon Programmers," as we navigate the challenges and learning opportunities in FRC programming.

## What is a Photon Programmer?

As everyone on Spectrum is part of Photon, everyone is considered to be learning at all times, even if they don't think they are. Programming is an empirical science; we are all figuring out problems through continuous testing, continuous reading, or continuous observation from within the team or other teams.

**Don't Limit Yourself**:
*   Search forums like Chief Delphi.
*   Ask mentors or other programmers for ideas.
*   Always ask questions!
*   **Embrace the Process**: Be ready to experience failure and success. Be prepared for uncertainty in outcomes.

## General Guidelines

### Shadow Programmers

Shadowing your fellow coders is not an optional part of learning.

*   **Observe and Learn**: You'll learn coding conventions and best practices by observing experienced programmers.
*   **Learn from Mistakes**: Learn from your programmers' mistakes, from your own mistakes, how you make mistakes, and strive not to repeat them.
*   **Invest in Learning**: The more effort you put into watching and learning to program, the more you can improve yourself and apply that knowledge to the team. Learn so that your future self can understand the needs of programming.

### Draw It Out

If you can't imagine your system or how a piece of code works:

*   **Visualize**: Find a similar analogous image to relate to, or draw the system's function out on a whiteboard, piece of paper, flowchart, or whatever visual aid works for you.
*   **Pre-planning**: Write notes on a notepad, piece of paper, or use `TODO` comments or Javadoc comments to outline the function of the program you're writing or the task you need to accomplish.

### 5 Second Rule

Patience and dedicated time to program are key.

*   **Thoughtful Responses**: Keep your responses well-thought-out instead of impulsive and reactive.
*   **Rubber Duck Debugging**: Define your system and ensure you understand the ongoing process, as if you were explaining it to a rubber duck.
*   **Be Merciful**: Be understanding with yourself when making mistakes; they are part of the learning process.
